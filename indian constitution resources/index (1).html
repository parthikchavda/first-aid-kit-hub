ભારતીય બંધારણ — 22 ભાગ (Parts) — કુલ 395 અનુ.
ભાગ I સંઘ અને રાજ્ય (અનુ. 1–4) ભારત = "Union of States" — ભારત રાજ્યોનો સંઘ છે
ભારત કોઈ "Federation" (ફેડરેશન) નથી, "Union of States" (રાજ્યોનો સંઘ) છે. એટલે રાજ્ય ભારતમાંથી અલગ ન થઈ શકે.
ભાગ II નાગરિકતા — Citizenship (અનુ. 5–11) Single Citizenship — ભારતમાં ફક્ત એક નાગરિકતા
USA જેવા દેશોમાં "Dual Citizenship" (રાજ્ય + દેશ) હોય છે, પણ ભારતમાં ફક્ત ભારતની નાગરિકતા.
ભાગ III મૂળભૂત અધિકારો — Fundamental Rights (અનુ. 12–35) Justiciable — Court દ્વારા apply કરી શકાય | અમેરિકાથી પ્રેરિત
અ. 21 — "Right to Life" — Police ગેરકાયદે ધરપકડ કરે તો SC/HC માં Writ દ્વારા ઈલાજ.
ભાગ IV DPSP — Directive Principles (અનુ. 36–51) Non-Justiciable — Court enforce ન કરી શકે | આયર્લેન્ડ પ્રેરિત
DPSP = સરકારને "ગુડ ગવર્નન્સ" માટે મળેલી ગાઈડ. Court enforce ન કરી શકે, પણ Moral duty. જેમ: School teacher ઘૂઘ ઘૂઘ ન ઘૂઘ ...
અ. 44 — UCC (Uniform Civil Code) — "one law for all" — DPSP છે, FR નહિ, એટલે Court force ન કરી શકે.
IV-A મૂળભૂત ફરજો — Fundamental Duties (અનુ. 51A) 42મો સુ. 1976 | રશિયા (USSR) પ્રેરિત | 11 ફરજો (86th Amend 2002 = 11th duty)

★★★ Kesavananda Bharati Case (1973) — SC 13 Judge Bench
Basic Structure Doctrine — Parliament can amend but NOT destroy basic structure. Federalism, Democracy, Secularism, Judicial Review = Basic Structure.
42nd Amendment = "Parliament is supreme, can change anything." → Kesavananda: "No! Basic Structure is permanent." Party change, not soul.
Parliament = "We remove Art. 32 (Writs)!" → Court: NO! Art. 32 = Basic Structure → Cannot remove.
★★★ Maneka Gandhi Case (1978) — Art. 21
Right to Life expanded — "Procedure established by Law" must be Fair, Just & Reasonable. Passport confiscate = violation. Expanded Art. 21 scope.
Passport जब्त → "Travel abroad" = Art. 21. पहले: AK Gopalan Case (1950) = Art. 21 narrow. Maneka = expanded. Now: Dignity, Privacy, Clean Environment = 21.
CCTV home के अंदर = Privacy violation → Art. 21 (Puttaswamy 2017 confirm).
★★★ S.R. Bommai Case (1994) — Art. 356
President's Rule (356) misuse control. Floor test must before recommending 356. SC can review 356 proclamation. Federalism = Basic Structure.
Gujarat 2002 → State government collapse fear → Governor 356 recommend. Bommai: Floor test first. 356 = last resort, not political tool.
★★★ Minerva Mills Case (1980) — Art. 368
42nd Amendment struck down partially. FR + DPSP balance must. Judicial review = Basic Structure. Parliament cannot give unlimited power to itself.
42nd Amend: DPSP > FR. Minerva: No balance! FR + DPSP both important. Court = watchdog, always.
★★ Golaknath Case (1967) — Art. 13
Parliament CANNOT amend Fundamental Rights. Later overruled by 24th Amendment + Kesavananda Case.
★★ Indra Sawhney Case (1992) — Reservation
Mandal Commission — OBC 27% reservation valid. Total reservation cap = 50%. Creamy Layer exclude from OBC. Economic criteria alone ≠ sufficient for SC/ST.
103rd Amendment (2019) = EWS 10% → But total = 60%. Case pending in SC for 50% cap review.
★★ Puttaswamy Case (2017) — Right to Privacy
Privacy = Fundamental Right under Art. 21. 9-judge bench unanimous. Aadhaar partially upheld.
Aadhaar = biometric data → Privacy concern → Puttaswamy case. SC: Privacy = FR, but Aadhaar = partially valid (welfare schemes).
★★ Hussainara Khatoon (1979) — 1st PIL
First Public Interest Litigation. Undertrial prisoners right. Free Legal Aid = FR. Speedy Trial = Art. 21.
PIL = Anyone can file on public behalf. 1979 से India में PIL system. Hussainara = Bihar undertrial prisoners years in jail without trial.
★★ Vishaka Case (1997) — Sexual Harassment
Workplace Sexual Harassment guidelines. Later POSH Act 2013. Art. 14, 19, 21 violation.
Bhanwari Devi case (Rajasthan) → Vishaka PIL → SC = workplace safety guidelines → POSH Act 2013.
★ A.K. Gopalan (1950) vs Maneka Gandhi (1978) — Art. 21
Gopalan: Art. 21 = narrow (procedure only). Maneka: Overruled — procedure must be Fair, Just, Reasonable. Art. 21 greatly expanded.
★ Berubari Union Case (1960) — Preamble
Preamble is NOT part of Constitution (later changed by Kesavananda = IS part). Territory cession needs Art. 368 amendment.
★ Kihoto Hollohan Case (1992) — Anti-Defection
Speaker/Chairman decision on Disqualification = judicial review possible. 10th Schedule (Anti-Defection) = valid.
MLA party change → Speaker disqualify → MLA says unfair → SC review possible (Kihoto).
